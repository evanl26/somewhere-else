About

Evan Lai is a photographer based in New York. He shoots on film and digital, usually somewhere he’s never been before, usually slower than everyone around him.

Why

I started carrying a camera because I kept forgetting things — not big things, but the small ones. The way light hits a canal at noon. A stranger pausing outside a flower shop. The feeling of a city before it wakes up.

Photography, for me, isn’t about the shot. It’s about having a reason to stop. Most people move through a place and let it wash over them. I’d rather stay a little longer, look a little closer, and bring something back.

Unhurried is that something.
